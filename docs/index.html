Community Mobility and COVID-19

Where have you been during the outbreak?

What is community mobility?

As global communities responded to COVID-19, Google heard from public health officials that the same type of aggregated,
anonymized insights they used in products such as Google Maps would be helpful as officials made critical decisions to combat COVID-19.

These Community Mobility Reports released by Google aimed to provide insights into what changed in response to policies
aimed at combating COVID-19. The reports charted movement trends over time by geography, across six different categories of places.

Grocery & pharmacy

Mobility trends for places like grocery store, farmers markets, food shops, drug stores, and pharmacies.

Parks

Mobility trends for places like local parks, national parks, public beaches, dog parks, plazas, and public gardens.

Transit stations

Mobility trends for places like public transport hubs such as subway, bus, and train stations.

Retail & recreation

Mobility trends for places like restaurants, cafes, shopping centers, theme parks, museums, and theaters.

Residential

Mobility trends for places of residence.


Workplaces

Mobility trends for places of work.
